# Chapter 6 Exam Review Outline — International Trade

## Core ideas
- Compare domestic autarky price with the world price.
- A small open economy takes the world price as given.
- If world price exceeds domestic autarky price, the country exports.
- If world price is below domestic autarky price, the country imports.
- Trade changes consumer surplus, producer surplus, and total surplus.
- Tariffs and quotas restrict trade and create deadweight loss.

## Key definitions
- **Domestic price**: the equilibrium price in the country if there is no international trade.
- **World price**: the equilibrium price on the international market.
- **Small open economy**: an economy small enough that its own demand or supply does not affect the world price.
- **Closed economy / autarky**: an economy that does not trade internationally.
- **Gains from trade**: the increase in total surplus in the open economy compared with autarky.

## Small open economy logic
- The country is a price taker in world markets.
- Domestic sellers will not accept less than the world price if they can sell abroad.
- Domestic buyers will not pay more than the world price if they can buy abroad.
- With free trade, domestic price equals world price: <code>P = Pw</code>.

## Exporting case
- If <code>Pd < Pw</code>, the country becomes an exporter.
- Domestic price rises from autarky price to world price.
- Quantity supplied rises.
- Quantity demanded falls.
- Exports = domestic supply at world price − domestic demand at world price.
- Domestic producers gain.
- Domestic consumers lose.
- Total surplus rises because producer gains exceed consumer losses.

## Importing case
- If <code>Pd > Pw</code>, the country becomes an importer.
- Domestic price falls from autarky price to world price.
- Quantity demanded rises.
- Quantity supplied falls.
- Imports = domestic demand at world price − domestic supply at world price.
- Domestic consumers gain.
- Domestic producers lose.
- Total surplus rises because consumer gains exceed producer losses.

## Extra benefits from trade
- Greater variety of goods for consumers.
- Producers can exploit economies of scale by selling into larger markets.
- Foreign competition can reduce domestic market power.
- Ideas and technology spread more quickly.

## Tariffs
- **Import tariff**: a tax on imported goods.
- In a small importing economy, a tariff raises the domestic price above the world price by the amount of the tariff:
  - <code>Pdomestic = Pw + t</code>
- Effects of a tariff:
  - domestic consumers lose
  - domestic producers gain
  - government gains tariff revenue
  - imports fall
  - total surplus falls
  - deadweight loss appears
- Reasons governments may impose tariffs:
  - protect domestic interest groups
  - strategic or defence reasons
  - raise government revenue

## Quotas
- **Import quota**: a legal limit on the quantity that may be imported.
- A quota reduces imports and raises the domestic price.
- Domestic consumers lose.
- Domestic producers gain.
- Import quota holders may gain quota rents / importer bonus.
- Total surplus falls and deadweight loss appears.

## Exam workflow
1. Find autarky equilibrium if needed.
2. Compare domestic price with world price.
3. Decide exporter or importer.
4. Under free trade set domestic price equal to world price.
5. Compute domestic quantity demanded and supplied at world price.
6. Compute imports or exports as the gap.
7. For a tariff, raise domestic price by the tariff amount and recompute.
8. Evaluate changes in consumer surplus, producer surplus, government revenue, and deadweight loss.

## Worked importing example from lecture style
- Demand: <code>P = 80 - Q</code>
- Supply: <code>P = Q</code>
- Autarky equilibrium:
  - <code>80 - Q = Q</code>
  - <code>Q = 40</code>, <code>P = 40</code>
- World price: <code>Pw = 30</code>
- Free trade:
  - Demand at 30: <code>Qd = 50</code>
  - Supply at 30: <code>Qs = 30</code>
  - Imports: <code>50 - 30 = 20</code>
- Surplus:
  - autarky TS = <code>800 + 800 = 1600</code>
  - free trade TS = <code>1250 + 450 = 1700</code>
  - gains from trade = <code>100</code>

## Exam traps
- In a small open economy, the domestic price equals the world price under free trade.
- Export quantity is supply minus demand, not the other way around.
- Import quantity is demand minus supply.
- A tariff creates government revenue, but a quota usually creates quota rents instead.
- Even if some domestic groups gain from trade restrictions, total surplus falls due to deadweight loss.
